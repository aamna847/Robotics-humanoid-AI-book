# Assessment Rubrics for Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

This document provides detailed rubrics for assessing student performance in the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics curriculum. These rubrics align with the learning outcomes and provide consistent evaluation standards.

## General Assessment Principles

- All assessments are competency-based
- Students must demonstrate practical application of concepts
- Code quality and documentation are integral to evaluation
- Safety and ethical considerations are evaluated as part of all assessments

## Chapter Assessment Rubrics

### Chapter 1: Physical AI Foundations (Weeks 1-2)

#### Learning Objectives Assessed:
- Understanding of embodied intelligence concepts
- Knowledge of sensor types and capabilities
- Basic understanding of humanoid robotics principles

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| Conceptual Understanding | Demonstrates comprehensive understanding of embodied intelligence with ability to explain complex relationships | Shows strong understanding of core concepts with minor gaps | Understands basic concepts but has some significant gaps | Shows limited understanding with major misconceptions |
| Sensor Knowledge | Identifies and explains multiple sensor types with their applications and limitations | Identifies most sensor types with good understanding of applications | Identifies basic sensor types with some understanding of applications | Limited identification of sensor types |
| Humanoid Principles | Thoroughly explains humanoid robotics principles with examples of applications | Explains core principles with relevant examples | Explains basic principles with limited examples | Struggles to explain basic principles |
| Communication | Articulates ideas clearly using proper terminology | Communicates well with mostly appropriate terminology | Communicates with some unclear areas | Communication is unclear or inappropriate |

- ROS 2 architecture (nodes, topics, services)
- URDF modeling

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|

## Capstone Project Rubric

### Learning Objectives Assessed:
- Integration of all curriculum concepts
- Independent problem-solving
- Professional quality implementation

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| Integration | Completely integrates all curriculum components seamlessly | Integrates most curriculum components effectively | Integrates several curriculum components | Limited integration of curriculum components |
| Problem-Solving | Demonstrates sophisticated problem-solving with innovative solutions | Shows good problem-solving with effective solutions | Demonstrates basic problem-solving | Limited problem-solving capability |
| Implementation Quality | Professional-quality implementation with excellent documentation | Good quality implementation with adequate documentation | Basic implementation with limited documentation | Poor implementation with minimal documentation |

## Assessment Weights

- Chapter Exercises: 40%
- Projects: 35%
- Capstone Project: 25%

## Grading Scale

- A: 90-100% (Excellent performance across multiple criteria)
- B: 80-89% (Good performance with some areas of excellence)
- C: 70-79% (Satisfactory performance meeting basic requirements)
- D: 60-69% (Minimal passing performance with significant weaknesses)
- F: Below 60% (Unsatisfactory performance failing to meet basic requirements)

## Special Considerations

- Group project elements are assessed individually
- Students may revise and resubmit work within specified deadlines
- Professional behavior and safety practices affect final grades
- Ethical considerations in AI and robotics are evaluated in all assessments
